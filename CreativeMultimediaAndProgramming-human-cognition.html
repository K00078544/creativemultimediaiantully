Human Congitiona and the influence of technology

limits of human cognition

There is a curiouHuman Congitiona and the influence of technologys dance taking place between humans and machines. We create the machines, and then adapt to them. Then what we create changes, and we adapt some more. The dance continues, with continuous change and adaption. Anastasia Utesheva describes her work studying the evolution of human cognition and behaviour in conjunction with our technological creations.

What you think of when you think of evolution? I used to think about it purely in a biological sense; species inheriting traits, developing traits that differed from previous generations, and so on. And then I thought about humans. Biologically we haven't really evolved for quite some time, yet huge archives of historical evidence show how human beings have evolved cognitively. We changed through the technologies we used which shaped our cultures and helped us create, spread and refine information. My research is on this process. How does it occur and why should anyone care?

Consider digital media. From static websites to those with which you can interact, to the ones that seem to read your mind before you first click. Every generation and type of digital media artefact has helped people transition cognitively into the next stage and a new phase of technology. How? Well, we learn through experience. New technologies create new experiences, new experiences drive new perspectives, new perspectives shape new technologies. Before social media I bet few people thought of their friends as networks or trophies. But then came Facebook.

So why do some experiences exist right now and not others? To find out I went to the source. I learned how digital artefacts are created through an ethnography at three companies in Sydney and Melbourne which produce digital media. I found a pattern. The digital products they make share the themes, values, ideals and logic with the people who created them. In other words, they inherit traits. Why should anyone care? Well, because of digital artefacts humans now have some amazing new experiences. Transcending temporal and spatial limits of our bodies is quite common. You may have experienced this before. This happens when you make a Skype call.

Now, imagine what other experiences we could unlock if we really put our minds to it. The potential is almost limitless. So technology shapes our evolution and we shape technologies’ evolution. Maybe if we understood this process better we could shape our evolutionary trajectory in the direction that we want to go. The best part, no one is excluded. Everyone who has ever used or created a technology is responsible for driving the evolution of our species. So I want to leave you with this; where we go next is up to you.
