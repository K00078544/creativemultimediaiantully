Tannoy Reintroduces 1970's Model Speakers

Annie Anglim 22/2/17

Why re-invent the wheel? this seems to be the motto at Tannoy who recently brought back to life their nearly five decade old Prestige range. Following on from this Tannoy have decided to give todays listener three models from their 1970's Legacy series

If we step back to the age when rock was at its prime due to the hippie counter culture of the 60's, hard rock bands such as The Rolling Stones, Lynard Skynard and Black Sabbath introduced listeners to a much heavier sound than what was already out there. Black artists who came to be recognised for Soul and Funk during the late 60's and early 70's were now producing dance music in the form of disco, and Punk was born through bands like the Stooges, The Clash and The Sex Pistols. for such an important decade in music history Tannoy had to be at the top of their game when it came to how this music was translated to its fans at home.

The speakers ranges being reintroduced are the Arden, Cheviot and Eaton. The Legacy range are produced in Scotland UK and feature Tannoy's Dual Concentric Drivers based on the 1974 HPD technology. These drivers are housed in high density cabinets.

The Eaton is the smallest speaker in the revamped range with dimentions of 52 x 35 x 25cm, 400w power handling and sensitivty of 87.5dB, next is the Cheviot floorstander dimentions 85 x 44 x 26cm, 500w power handling and 89dB sensitivity. The largest model is the floorstanding Arden at 99 x 66 x 37cm and can handle a whopping 600w with 91dB sensitivity.

All three speakers feature an energy control for fine tuning the speakers for the perfect listening experience.

All come with gold plated, biwirable binding posts with an extra post, providing grounding whic reduces radio interference and improves midrange clarity.
